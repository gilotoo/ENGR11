Project 2 - Combining Multiple Designs

The objective of this project was to use our understanding of additive and subtractive manufacturing to create a design model using multiple designs off a public space. We were tasked to combine designs to make the new design better than the original.

Design Search and Sketch

The first step of the design process was to find designs on a public website that I could use to create a new one. I browsed through the website Thingaverse and saw some cool designs I liked. I decided to use the design of a bottle sleeve for cans and bottles. I came up with the idea to add another design of a bottle opener to add to the bottle sleeve for convenience. I sketched out my idea and when I found designs that would fit my idea, I exported them to the CAD software TinkerCad.

First Design

Once I got my two designs (bottle sleeve and opener) on tinkercad, I began combining the designs together. I isolated the opener section of the bottle opener by removing the handle of the bottle opener. Next, I placed the opener on the bottle sleeve in the same orientation I had in my design sketch. Once I liked the design I created, I exported it to Prusa Slicer software to be printed. I kept the default printer settings on Prusa and generated support material to help the print.

Problems with First Design

I ran into a couple problems with the design of my first prototype. First, the diimensions of the interior opening of the bottle sleeve did not fit the sizing of a regular glass bottle. The design of the original sleeve had an oblique opening that I did not see originally. Secondly, the height of the sleeve was too short and that caused stability issues when a bottle was inside the sleeve. Third, the opener was not sturdy enough to use for a bottle top and it was not there was a lack of comfortability when using the sleeve because of the opener's posiiton.

Second Design

I went back to the drawing board and fixed the issues that the first design had. I fixed the opening of the sleeve to fit the regular size glass bottle by making the diameter 2.5 inches. I increased the height of the bottle sleeve from three to six inches. I also took the bottle opener portion and flushed it to the sleeve, making it engraved in the bottle rather than sticking out awkwardly.

Final Result

Once I was comfortbale with my changes, I exported the file to Prusa Slicer and kept the default printer settings. The only thing I changed in the Prusa software was the generated support. I sliced the print and placed it on a Prusa Mini+ printer. Unfortunately, the print failed and I was not able to get a final tangible design. However, I am still workking out the issues and figuring out why the print failed.

Reflection

Overall, this project was a very open-ended one that had a lot of steps along the way. The first design of my print came out successful and served some purpose but could have been better. The second design on CAD was an improvement but struggled in its transfer to the Prusa software and physical print. In the future, I would optimize my design by including a handle for the bottle sleeve so the user can have a better grip on it. I would also add insulation material to the inside of the sleeve to keep the drink of choice at a cold temperature.
